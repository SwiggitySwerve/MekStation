# Proposal: add-campaign-fast-forward-api

## Why

**W3 of the Seam-First Ladder** (council decision `openspec/council-decisions/2026-07-09-ui-audit-testability-architecture.md`): time-to-state is the bottleneck for exercising campaign subsystems — nothing today jumps a campaign to "week 3, two battles fought, contract paid, repair bay busy" without a human clicking through days. The council allocated headless coverage as dominant **only for the seam-free subsystems** (Phase 3 update 4): economy ledger, XP/skill math, and maintenance progression — subsystems whose worst failure modes are arithmetic and bookkeeping drift, not mount/handoff seams. This change builds that headless layer: `fastForwardCampaign()` in `src/lib/campaign/fastForward/`, a jest-speed driver that advances a campaign N in-fiction days through the production day pipeline, fights bridged AtB scenarios through the real API handlers and the real `GameEngine`, and asserts economy/XP/maintenance invariants over the result. It is also the pack-minting engine W4 (`scenario-packs`) builds on: W4's `campaign/*.campaign.json` packs are `buildSerializedCampaign` dumps of W3 runs, so hand-authored-envelope drift dies by construction.

Three verified coverage gaps motivate the scope (all re-verified against `main@bea54767c`, which includes the W1 SP-determinism implementation):

- **The engine→campaign chain has zero continuous coverage — and authoring this change proved the chain is actually severed.** Every existing test that asserts post-battle campaign state — `src/__tests__/integration/campaignCombatLoop.test.ts`, `phase4CampaignRoundTrip.test.ts`, `src/lib/campaign/processors/__tests__/postBattleProcessor.retryIdempotency.test.ts` — hand-constructs its `ICombatOutcome` fixture and never invokes `GameEngine`. Conversely, every test that runs `GameEngine.runToCompletion` for real (`GameEngine.test.ts`, `combat4v4JourneyProof.test.ts`, `phase1Capstone.test.ts`, `campaignMissionEncounterLaunchIntegrity.test.ts`) asserts combat mechanics only and never touches campaign roster XP, ledger, or repair state. Worse: the production chain **cannot** grant per-battle XP from an engine-derived outcome today. Derived deltas key `unitId` by session unit id (`${side}-${slot}-${unitRef}`, `preBattleSessionBuilder.ts:83-89` via `deriveCombatOutcome`), while `postBattleProcessor` resolves roster entries with `entriesByPilotId.get(delta.unitId)` keyed on vault `pilotId` (`postBattleProcessor.ts:67,79`), and `IUnitCombatDelta` carries no pilot linkage (`CombatOutcome.ts:81-101`) — every green XP test passes only by hand-rigging `unitId === pilotId` (`'unit-A'` in `phase4CampaignRoundTrip.test.ts:236`, `campaignCombatLoop.test.ts:136`). That is the dual-id-construction class (#1019 family) in the flesh, discovered by authoring the very change meant to catch it. This change therefore carries **one narrowly sanctioned production fix** (see What Changes) so the XP invariants assert what production actually grants instead of rigging fixtures into assertion theater. **Correction to the council brief:** the council's "XP has ZERO coverage today" framing is inaccurate as stated — `xpAwards` has 37 unit cases, `XPCalculator` 17, `skillCostTraits` a 427-line suite, and `phase4CampaignRoundTrip.test.ts` asserts `campaignXpEarned > 0` through a real `advanceDay()`. The real, narrower gap is the missing **engine-driven continuous chain** (now known to be severed, not merely untested), which is precisely what this change closes; restating "zero coverage" would be checkable-and-false.
- **The 0-damage/instant-defeat bug class recurred twice** — commit `5ced64549` (#998: BattleMechs seeded at 0/0 armor+structure, one-hit kills, both record sheet and damage pipeline reading empty maps) and commit `91104d43a` (#1019: mirrored canonical `unitRef`s colliding in the session-state reducer, Defeat-by-elimination declared after Initiative with zero combat events). Both shipped while jest was green because no suite watched for "a battle completed but nobody took damage." This change ships `assertSessionInflictedDamage` (verified: the name exists nowhere in `src/` today) as a standing guard applied to every fast-forwarded battle.
- **AtB scenario generation is dark headlessly — and triple-gated, not double-gated.** Scenario generation runs only on Mondays, only when `campaign.options.useAtBScenarios` is true — and that option defaults to **false** and is absent from `createDefaultCampaignOptions` (`src/types/campaign/options/combatForceOptions.ts:76-83`) — and, past both gates, each combat team must still win a **d100 battle-chance roll** against its role's base chance (`checkForBattle`, `src/lib/campaign/scenario/battleChance.ts:120-138`; force-derived teams all get FRONTLINE's 20%, `scenarioGenerationProcessor.ts:93-97`, base table `battleChance.ts:50-58`). A mis-built fixture silently generates nothing, and even a correct fixture can roll zero battles on a sparse roll budget — a pinned seed that happens to pass is a de-facto seed→outcome dependency that any upstream draw inserted into the daily stream silently flips. This change's fixture and expectation contracts make both failure modes loud AND make the guarantee seed-independent: the acceptance fixture must enable AtB, cover ≥1 Monday, and declare explicit combat teams whose roll budget (teams × Mondays × per-roll chance) makes zero bridged scenarios practically impossible for any seed; the run must fail loud if zero scenarios bridge, naming the battle-chance gate.

**Council-sketch drift, resolved here (verified facts override the sketch):** the council row's literal wiring — "`bridgedEncounters` → `materializeCampaignMissionEncounter`" — does not match production code. `materializeCampaignMissionEncounter`'s single production call site is the roster-driven mission launch flow (`src/pages-modules/gameplay/campaigns/missionLaunchPage.launch.ts:232`); `campaign.bridgedEncounters`' production consumer is `launchCampaignEncounter` (`src/lib/campaign/encounter/launchCampaignEncounter.ts`), a direct-call service path with no fetch boundary — and even that path only creates a shell `GameSession` (via `buildGameUnitsFromEncounter`, DEFAULT-skill, non-catalog-adapted) that never reaches `GameEngine`. No production session-to-engine bridge exists for bridged scenarios today. The design (`design.md` D2) resolves this explicitly: the fast-forward combat runner drives the **mission materializer's REST surface through the real handlers** (the only reusable-unchanged, fetch-injectable materialization path) and mirrors the **only** production chain that actually runs `GameEngine.runToCompletion` end-to-end (`usePreBattleLaunch.ts:186-262`: `buildPreparedBattleData` → engine → derive+publish outcome), with the trade-offs recorded.

## What Changes

- **`fastForwardCampaign()` driver** (`src/lib/campaign/fastForward/`): advances a loaded campaign N in-fiction days by looping the campaign store's `advanceDay` action (the registered day-pipeline path — `registerBuiltinProcessors()` + `getDayPipeline().processDay()`, never the legacy registry-free `dayAdvancement.ts` fallback), establishes the store's combat-outcome-bus subscription before any battle, detects newly bridged scenarios per day, hands them to the combat runner, and returns a machine-readable run report (days advanced, day reports, scenarios generated/bridged, battles fought with ids and seeds, outcome-application status). Declared run expectations (minimum scenarios bridged, minimum battles fought) throw on miss — never a silently green no-op run.
- **In-process API router** (`fetchImpl` shim): a `fetch`-shaped adapter that imports the **real** `/api/forces` and `/api/encounters` Pages Router handler modules and invokes them directly via `node-mocks-http` against the real SQLite service (`:memory:` in jest) — the exact pattern already proven by `src/__tests__/api/forces/forceMultiAssignment.real.test.ts`. Never mocks, never a parallel re-implementation: handler drift breaks the fast-forward loudly, the maintenance surface the council accepted.
- **Combat runner**: per bridged scenario — build the roster projection, materialize player force + opponent force + encounter through `materializeCampaignMissionEncounter` **unchanged** (its `fetchImpl` parameter already exists) with the in-process router, build combat units through the same catalog-adapted construction path the live SP launch uses (`buildPreparedBattleData`), run `GameEngine.runToCompletion` with a seed derived deterministically from the campaign's persisted `rngSeed` + in-fiction day + scenario id (never `Date.now()`), and publish the derived outcome with campaign linkage through the production combat-outcome bus so the **next** day's `postBattleProcessor` applies it.
- **Sanctioned production fix — engine-derived outcome pilot attribution** (the one production touch this change carries, discovered during authoring): `IUnitCombatDelta` gains an optional `pilotRef` populated at derivation from `IGameUnit.pilotRef` (`deriveCombatOutcome`/`unitToDelta`), and `postBattleProcessor` resolves roster entries and vault pilots by that linkage when present, falling back to the legacy `unitId` key when absent (so persisted outcomes and existing hand-built-fixture tests keep working unmodified). Kill attribution stays keyed by session unit id against the report rows. Without this, engine-derived outcomes grant zero pilot XP (dual-id severance, see Why) and the XP capstone would be unimplementable or rigged.
- **`assertSessionInflictedDamage`**: a standing invariant guard applied to every fast-forwarded battle — fails loud on zero-armor battle starts (#998 shape), zero-cumulative-damage completed battles (deltas-unchanged alone trips it; damage events present alongside unchanged deltas are named as the phantom-damage id-mismatch signature, never absolution), and terminal outcomes without justifying combat events (#1019 shape).
- **Economy / XP / maintenance invariant assertions**: ledger balance reconciliation (per-transaction-type signed), contract payout exactly-once per terminal status flip and under same-match-id re-publication (production's actual guarantee — scenario generation is status-blind, so fixtures bound contract windows to keep a terminal contract from fighting again; the terminal-to-terminal re-payout is a documented production gap, out of scope), salary-path mutual exclusion, XP monotonicity + apply-once + participation-matched increments through engine-driven combat with production pilot attribution, repair-hour daily bounds, max-cap clamps, and ticket idempotency across re-runs.
- **Determinism contract**: two runs of the identical fixture and day count produce equal invariant-level end states. Campaign day-processor RNG is already deterministic per audit D-10 (`src/lib/campaign/utils/campaignRng.ts`); the one open gap was the engine-seed derivation point, which this change closes with the `QuickResolveService` precedent (own seeded engine per battle). No golden traces — run-to-run equality only, wall-clock-bearing fields excluded.
- **Live-parity acceptance** (council open risk 1): the fast-forwarded end state is compared against a discrete-step live-equivalent jest run — the same production primitives driven one player-paced step at a time, **independently hand-inlined**: the live-equivalent never imports the fast-forward driver or combat runner (shared surface limited to the fixture builder, in-process router, and seed helper), both sides advance the same total in-fiction days ending on the same in-fiction date, and at least one player-realistic ordering variation (a fight deferred ≥1 day) is asserted equal — on the seam invariant set (unit survival, armor/structure deltas, balance delta, transaction types/amounts, XP counters, scenario/contract statuses, repair-ticket counts/kinds). The acceptance certifies the fast-forward orchestration, not the shared primitives (those pass both sides identically and are covered by their own suites — stated honestly in the spec). Divergence fails the acceptance and, per the council's recorded consequence, keeps scenario packs triage-only permanently. Invariants, never golden traces.
- **Ledger**: `openspec/active-change-ledger.json` gains this change's entry (ships with this declarative PR, per the W1/W2 precedent).

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `campaign-fast-forward-api`: the headless fast-forward driver contract — day-loop semantics, fixture/expectation fail-loud rules, real-handler + real-engine battle execution, `assertSessionInflictedDamage`, economy/XP/maintenance invariants over fast-forwarded runs, deterministic reproducibility from the campaign seed, live-parity acceptance methodology, and the complements-never-replaces rule against the W2 seam anchors. Scoped narrowly to the orchestration/driver layer: it does NOT re-specify the bridge, materialization, launch, or outcome-enqueue mechanics that `campaign-combat-loop` owns, nor the engine determinism contracts `simulation-system` owns.

### Modified Capabilities

- `campaign-combat-loop`: (1) the "Campaign-Linked Encounter Launch" requirement gains a transport-agnostic clause — the materialization contract (roster parity, opponent sizing, unresolvable-unit blocking) holds identically whether the `/api/forces` + `/api/encounters` handlers are reached through live browser fetch or through an in-process fetch implementation invoking the same handler modules directly. This is the delta that gives the fast-forward path doctrinal cover to reuse `materializeCampaignMissionEncounter` unchanged. (2) A new ADDED requirement, "Engine-Derived Outcome Pilot Attribution", specifies the sanctioned production fix: derived unit deltas carry pilot linkage, and post-battle application resolves roster pilots by it (unit-id fallback preserved) — closing the dual-id severance between session unit ids and roster pilot ids.

## Impact

- **New code** (implementation phase, all additive): `src/lib/campaign/fastForward/` module tree (driver, in-process API router, combat runner, `assertSessionInflictedDamage`, invariant assertion helpers, campaign fixture builder) plus integration suites under `src/__tests__/integration/` (capstone run, determinism run-twice, live-parity acceptance) and colocated unit tests. No new jest project is needed: `jest.config.js` defines only `unit` and `a11y` projects, and `src/__tests__/integration/` files are matched by the `unit` project's generic `__tests__` glob (verified) — the suites join the existing lane automatically.
- **Modified code**: exactly one sanctioned production fix — outcome pilot attribution across three files: `src/types/combat/CombatOutcome.ts` (additive optional `pilotRef` on `IUnitCombatDelta`), `src/lib/combat/outcome/combatOutcome.ts` (populate it from `IGameUnit.pilotRef` at derivation), `src/lib/campaign/processors/postBattleProcessor.ts` (prefer pilot linkage for roster/vault resolution, `unitId` fallback preserved). Additive and backward-compatible: linkage-free outcomes (persisted data, existing hand-built fixtures) resolve exactly as today. Everything else — `materializeCampaignMissionEncounter` (fetch-injectable), `scenarioEncounterBridgeProcessor`, the day pipeline, `GameEngine`, and the combat-outcome bus — is consumed as-is. Any further production touch must be additive-export-only and recorded in the tasks.
- **Specs**: new `openspec/specs/campaign-fast-forward-api/spec.md` (at archive time); delta to `openspec/specs/campaign-combat-loop/spec.md`. No `journey-qc` delta (verified: no browser/Playwright surface in this change's scope). No `simulation-system` delta (verified: `runToCompletion`'s headless/deterministic/batch contracts are already specified there; this change is a pure consumer).
- **Ordering**: implementation follows the ladder's strict sequencing — it starts after the W2 (`add-seam-trust-anchor-journeys`) implementation merges. Mechanically W3 depends only on W1's merged determinism work (already in this base: `config.seed` stamping on `runToCompletion` sessions).
- **Ledger reconciliation at ship time**: this worktree's `active-change-ledger.json` predates PR #1041 (which archived `add-sp-combat-determinism` on `origin/main`); the PR for this change must land its ledger entry on top of a rebase against current `origin/main` or `qc:openspec-ci:validate` fails the build.
- **Risk**: low-to-medium. Almost entirely test-support surface, plus one narrow additive production fix (pilot attribution — fallback-preserving, gated by its own unit tests and the existing hand-built-fixture suites staying green unmodified). The test surface imports real handlers, real SQLite, and real engine construction — the riskiest seams (handler invocation shim, store-singleton lifecycle in jest, layering of the `buildPreparedBattleData` import, attribution fallback) are individually gated in `design.md` (R1–R4, R10).

## Non-goals

- **No W4 scope**: no `e2e/scenario-packs/` registry, no pack manifest, no `loadCampaignPack`/`loadEncounterPack` loaders, no pack minting tasks — this change only guarantees the fast-forward runs W4 will later dump packs from.
- **The W2 seam anchors are untouched and never replaced.** Fast-forward coverage is never represented as covering the route-mount, recovery-rehydration, client-side materialization handoff, or fresh-construction seams; the anchors retain permanent authority (spec requirement). Per the council: headless shortcuts institutionalize the blind spot if they substitute for seam journeys.
- **No CI gating changes.** The new suites ride the existing jest `unit` lane via the `__tests__` glob; no `pr-checks.yml` edits, no required-check changes, no lane creation. Wiring headless invariants into PR gates is W6 scope.
- **No UI.** No pages, components, routes, or store-surface changes.
- **No production behavior changes beyond the one sanctioned pilot-attribution fix** (see Impact). The day pipeline, bridge, materializer, launch paths, engine, and outcome bus keep their exact semantics; this change adds an orchestrator around them.
- **No contract-closure production fix.** Authoring surfaced a real production gap: `getActiveContracts` is status-blind (`campaignCommandSelectors.ts:241-260`), so an already-terminal contract can fight again and a terminal-to-terminal status flip re-pays it (`applyContractClosure` has no once-only guard; `processedFulfilledContractIds` appends without a membership check). Fixing it is out of scope — fixtures bound contract windows instead (spec: Economy Ledger Invariants), and the gap is recorded in `design.md` (R9 / Open Questions) for a follow-up change.
- **No XP-spend driving.** The purchase surface (`purchaseSPA` / `POST /api/pilots/[id]/purchase-ability`) is manual/out-of-band and stays out of the fast-forward loop; XP monotonicity is asserted on that basis.
- **No scenario-composition-faithful OpFor.** The mission materializer stamps its own deterministic opponent selection and generic map/victory config rather than the bridge's BV-matched `opForConfig` and scenario-derived conditions; fast-forwarded battles are campaign-linkage-faithful, not scenario-composition-faithful (design D2 trade-off, recorded as a possible future change).
- **No stream-continuation or golden-trace determinism claims.** Same rules as W1: invariants across runs, never seed→outcome tables.
